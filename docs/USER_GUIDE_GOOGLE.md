# GrowthPath — Google Interview Preparation Guide

A step-by-step navigation guide for users preparing for Google interviews.

---

## Step 1: Register

**Page:** Login Screen -> Click **"Register"**

1. Fill in your **Full Name** and **Email**
2. Create a **Password**
3. Select **Google** as your target company
4. Pick your **Role** (e.g., SDE, SDET, DevOps)
5. Pick your **Level** (e.g., L3, L4, L5, L6)
6. Choose your **Tech Stack** (e.g., Python, Java, React)
7. Choose your **Interest Areas** (e.g., System Design, Data Structures)
8. Set **Hours per Day** you can dedicate to study
9. Click **Register**

---

## Step 2: Login & Change Password

**Page:** Login Screen

1. Enter your **email** and **password**
2. Click **Login**
3. On first login, you will be prompted to **change your password**
4. Set a new password and confirm
5. After changing, you are automatically taken to the **Quick Assessment**

---

## Step 3: Quick Assessment

**Page:** Quick Assessment Screen (auto-redirect after password change)

1. You will see **5-10 Google-specific Phone Screen questions**
2. Answer each question using **text** or **voice** (click the mic icon)
3. A timer tracks your time per question
4. Click **Submit** after answering all questions
5. Your results will show:
   - **Starting ELO rating** (e.g., 1350)
   - **Google L5 hiring bar** (e.g., 1800)
   - **Weaknesses identified** from your answers
6. A **personalized learning path** is generated from your weak areas
7. Click **Continue** to go to the Dashboard

> **Tip:** You can skip the Quick Assessment, but taking it gives you a more accurate starting point and a better learning path.

---

## Step 4: Dashboard — Your Home Base

**Page:** Dashboard (NavBar -> **"Dashboard"** or the GrowthPath logo)

The Dashboard shows your complete progress at a glance:

| Section | What You See |
|---------|-------------|
| **Hero Section** | Your target (Google + Role + Level), main action button |
| **ELO Rating** | Your current ELO vs Google's hiring bar with progress ring |
| **Plan Progress** | Percentage of learning plan completed |
| **Skill Gap Map** | Your strengths vs areas that need improvement, with priority levels |
| **Mock History Chart** | Bar chart of your last 5 mock interviews with ELO changes |
| **Sub-ELO Breakdown** | Individual scores for Coding, System Design, Behavioral, etc. |
| **Learning Path Timeline** | Ordered list of topics with status, deadlines, and ratings |

**Key Actions on Dashboard:**
- **"Continue Learning: [Topic Name]"** — Jump to your next learning topic
- **"Start Mock Interview"** — Begin a full 5-round Google mock interview
- **"Resume Learning"** — Continue from where you left off

---

## Step 5: Learn

**Navigation:** Click **"Learn"** in the top navigation bar

1. You will see your **Learning Path** — topics ordered by weakness priority
2. Click on any **topic** to start studying
3. Each topic has **4 learning dimensions**:
   - **Interview Prep** — Common interview questions and approaches
   - **Implementation** — Hands-on coding and design patterns
   - **Examples** — Real-world examples and case studies
   - **Best Practices** — Industry standards and tips
4. Read through each dimension's content (rendered as formatted text with code examples)
5. Use **Bookmark** to save your position and return later
6. Click **"Mark Complete"** on each dimension as you finish it
7. Use **"Mark for Review"** on topics you want to revisit later
8. When all 4 dimensions are complete, take the **Topic Assessment**

### Taking a Topic Assessment

1. After completing a topic's learning content, click **"Take Assessment"**
2. Answer **5 questions** on the topic
3. Each answer is scored 1-5 based on quality
4. View your **matched keywords** and **missed keywords**
5. Use **"Show Answer"** to see the model answer (50% score penalty)
6. Your topic rating is calculated from all 5 answers

---

## Step 6: Start a Mock Interview

**Navigation:** Dashboard -> Click **"Start Mock Interview"**

### Before the Mock
- The system checks if you can start a mock (1 full mock per day)
- Questions are freshly generated for Google at your level

### During the Mock — 5 Rounds (30 minutes total)

| Round | Type | Answer Mode | What to Expect |
|-------|------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Phone Screen** | Text (code) | Coding and algorithm questions |
| 2 | **System Design** | Text + Voice | Architecture and scalability questions |
| 3 | **Behavioral** | Voice | STAR format questions about your experience |
| 4 | **Domain** | Text + Voice | Role-specific technical questions |
| 5 | **Bar Raiser** | Voice | Cross-functional and leadership questions |

### How Each Round Works

1. A **question** is displayed with a **countdown timer**
2. Type or speak your answer depending on the round's answer mode
3. The **AI Interviewer** (with a Google interviewer persona) will:
   - Acknowledge your answer briefly
   - Ask **follow-up probes** to test depth (up to 5 levels deep)
   - Probe harder if your answer is strong, guide you if struggling
4. You can click **"Get Hint"** if stuck (small score penalty applies)
5. The **conversation depth indicator** shows your current probe level (1-5)
6. When the timer runs out or you finish, the round **auto-advances** to the next one
7. A **transition screen** shows between rounds

> **Tip:** The deeper you go in probe levels, the better your score. Aim for Level 4-5 on each question.

---

## Step 7: Review Your Mock Results

**Page:** Mock Results (shown automatically after completing the mock)

### What You See After the Mock

#### 1. Scorecard — Round-by-Round Breakdown
- Each round scored on:
  - **Thinking Process** (60% weight) — How you approached the problem
  - **Accuracy** (40% weight) — Correctness of your answer
- Sub-scores for each dimension

#### 2. ELO Change
- Your ELO before and after the mock
- Example: **"ELO 1350 -> 1420 (+70)"**
- Animated counter shows the change

#### 3. Rubric Reveal
- For each question, see **what the interviewer was looking for**
- Checklist shows which rubric items you **covered** and which you **missed**
- Missed items link to relevant **learning content** ("Learn about Caching Strategy ->")

#### 4. Hiring Committee Simulation
- **3 virtual interviewers** review your performance
- Each interviewer:
  - Evaluates their assigned rounds
  - Gives a **HIRE** or **NO HIRE** verdict
  - Provides a **quoted reason** referencing your specific answers
- **Final committee verdict** based on majority vote

#### 5. Learning Path Update
- Your learning path **automatically reorders** based on mock weaknesses
- Weak areas move up in priority
- A notification shows the before/after comparison

**Actions After Results:**
- **"Retake Mock"** — If you got NO HIRE, try again (next day)
- **"Back to Dashboard"** — Return to see your updated progress

## Step 8: Track Progress & Repeat

**Navigation:** Use the **NavBar** to move between sections

| NavBar Item | What It Does |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Dashboard** | See ELO progress, gap map, learning path, mock history |
| **Learn** | Study topics, take assessments |
| **Interview** | Legacy role-based or JD-based practice interview |
| **History** | View all past sessions with scores and dates |
| **Review** | Revisit topics you marked for review |
| **Profile** | Edit your settings, change company/role, regenerate learning plan |

### The Improvement Loop

```
Learn weak topics -> Take Mock Interview -> Review Results -> Repeat
     ^                                                          |
     |__________________________________________________________|
```

1. **Check Dashboard** — See your ELO and weakest areas
2. **Study** (Learn) — Focus on topics flagged as weak
3. **Take Assessment** — Test yourself on studied topics
4. **Take Mock Interview** — 1 per day, full Google simulation
5. **Review Results** — Understand rubric misses, committee feedback
6. **Repeat** — Learning path auto-adjusts each time

### Targeted Practice (Unlimited)
- Besides the daily mock, you can do **unlimited targeted practice** on specific round types
- Great for focusing on your weakest round (e.g., System Design only)

---

## Step 9: Interview Ready!

**Page:** Dashboard

When your **ELO reaches or exceeds Google's hiring bar**:
- **L3:** ELO >= 1500
- **L4:** ELO >= 1650
- **L5:** ELO >= 1800
- **L6:** ELO >= 1950

You will see a **celebration banner** on the Dashboard!

This means you are consistently performing at a level that would pass Google's interview bar based on your mock performance.

> **Tip:** Keep practicing even after hitting Interview Ready — consistency matters. Your ELO can drop if you take mocks and perform below your level.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|---------|
| Page is blank | Hard refresh with **Ctrl+Shift+R** |
| Can't start mock | You can only start **1 full mock per day**. Try targeted practice instead |
| Voice not working | Allow microphone permission in your browser. Check the mic indicator on the Interview setup screen |
| ELO not updating | ELO updates after completing a full mock or quick assessment. Learning alone doesn't change ELO |
| Learning path didn't change | Path reorders after mock interviews, not after learning sessions |
